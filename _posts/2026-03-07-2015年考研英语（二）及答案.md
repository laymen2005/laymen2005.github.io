## Section III: Translation (翻译)

**46. Translate the following text from English into Chinese.**

> Think about driving a route that's very familiar. It could be your commute to work, a trip into town or the way home. Whichever it is, you know every twist and turn like the back of your hand. On these sorts of trips it's easy to lose concentration on the driving and pay little attention to the passing scenery. The consequence is that you perceive that the trip has taken less time than it actually has.
>
> This is the well-travelled road effect: people tend to underestimate the time it takes to travel a familiar route.
>
> The effect is caused by the way we allocate our attention. When we travel down a well-known route, because we don't have to concentrate much, time seems to flow more quickly. And afterwards, when we come to think back on it, we can't remember the journey well because we didn't pay much attention to it. So we assume it was shorter.

**参考译文：**
想象一下驾驶在一条非常熟悉的路线上。这可能是你上班的通勤路、进城的一次出行或者是回家的路。无论是哪一种，你对每一个弯道的熟悉程度都了如指掌。在这类行程中，很容易分散对驾驶的注意力，而很少关注沿途的风景。结果就是，你觉得这次行程所花的时间比实际要少。

这就是“熟路效应”：人们倾向于低估行驶在熟悉路线上所花费的时间。

这种效应是由我们分配注意力的方式引起的。当我们行驶在一条熟悉的路线上时，因为不需要太集中注意力，时间似乎过得更快。事后，当我们回想起来时，由于当时没有太多关注，我们无法清晰地记得这段旅程。所以我们假设它更短。
